## 1. STRATEGIC OVERVIEW

### The Core Principle

Every interaction with BRUTAL. should either (a) deliver value or (b) naturally create desire for the next level of service. Never both at the same time — give value first, let the gap become obvious.

### The Hormozi Frame

Alex Hormozi's $100M Offers framework says the perfect offer maximizes **Dream Outcome** and **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** while minimizing **Time Delay** and **Effort & Sacrifice**. At every rung of this ladder, we're adjusting those four levers.

### The Brunson Frame

Russell Brunson's Value Ladder says: never ask someone to marry you on the first date. Start with a free taste, build trust through small transactions, then earn the right to make bigger asks. Each step must over-deliver so the next feels like a natural, even inevitable, decision.

### BRUTAL.'s Unfair Advantage

1. **The Demo Tool** — An AI-powered audit that gives instant, personalized value (already built at brutalmk.com)
2. **AEO Specialization** — Most Spanish agencies don't touch Answer Engine Optimization yet; BRUTAL. owns this niche
3. **Neobrutalist Brand** — Memorable, polarizing, impossible to ignore in a market of bland agencies
4. **4-Person Team** — Fast decisions, no bureaucracy, clients talk to the people doing the work

## RUNG 0: THE BAIT — FREE

### What It Is
The **Analizador BRUTAL.** — the AI-powered demo tool already on brutalmk.com that analyzes any business's digital presence and generates a 3-tab report:
- Tab 1: SEO & AEO Audit (Google + AI search engine visibility)
- Tab 2: Content Calendar Ideas (Instagram Reels, Stories, posts)
- Tab 3: Meta Ads Funnel Strategy (audience, budget, creative direction)

### What the User Gets (Free)
- Instant, personalized report with real data about their business
- 3–5 specific, actionable recommendations per tab
- A "BRUTAL. Score" from 0 to 100 rating their digital presence
- Comparison against industry benchmarks

### What BRUTAL. Gets
- Email address + business URL (required to run the tool)
- Business category, location, approximate revenue tier (dropdown during input)
- Segmentation data for follow-up sequences
- A lead who has already seen the gap between where they are and where they could be

### Psychological Triggers
- **Instant gratification** — Results in under 60 seconds, no waiting for a "consultant to get back to you"
- **Curiosity gap** — The report shows WHAT's wrong but not exactly HOW to fix it
- **Social proof** — Show counter: "3,247 negocios analizados en Espana"
- **Loss aversion** — "Tu competencia en [Ciudad] tiene un BRUTAL. Score de 73. Tu: 34."

### Capacity Requirement
- Fully automated, zero marginal cost per lead
- Antonio maintains the tool (~4 hrs/month)
- Budget: €200-400/month in Meta Ads + Google Ads driving traffic to the tool

### Key Metrics to Track
- Tool completions per month (target: 200+)
- Email capture rate (target: 80%+)
- Tripwire conversion rate (target: 5-10% of tool users)

## RUNG 1: THE TRIPWIRE — €47–€97 (One-Time)

### Purpose
Convert a free lead into a **paying customer** for the first time. The goal is NOT profit — it's changing the relationship from "lead" to "client." People who pay €47 are 10x more likely to buy a €1,500/month retainer than people who only consumed free content.

### Option A: "Informe BRUTAL. Pro" — €47
**What's included:**
- Everything from the free report, PLUS:
- 15-page deep-dive PDF specific to their business
- Keyword gap analysis (SEO) with exact search volumes
- AI visibility audit (which AI chatbots mention their competitors but not them)
- 30-day content calendar with specific post ideas, captions, and hashtags
- Meta Ads budget calculator with projected ROI for their sector
- 15-minute recorded Loom video walkthrough by a BRUTAL. team member explaining the findings

**Delivery:** Automated report generation + 1 manual Loom video (~20 min of team time per order)

**Margin:** ~85% (€40 profit per sale, mostly covers Loom recording time)

### Option B: "Taller BRUTAL." — €97
**What's included:**
- 90-minute live group workshop (max 15 businesses per session, run 2x/month)
- Topic rotates: "SEO para Pymes que No Saben SEO" / "Crea tu Calendario de Contenido en 90 Minutos" / "Meta Ads: De 0 a Tu Primera Campana Rentable"
- Attendees leave with a completed deliverable (their own content calendar, their first ad campaign draft, etc.)
- Recording available for 30 days
- 10-minute 1-on-1 "hot seat" at the end for 3 attendees (chosen by lottery)

**Delivery:** 90 min of Xino or Jose's time + 30 min prep = 2 hours per session. At 10 attendees x €97 = €970 per session.

**Margin:** ~90% (€873 profit per session)

### How It's Sold
1. **Exit-intent popup** on the free tool: "Quieres el informe completo? Solo €47."
2. **Email sequence** (5 emails over 7 days after free report):
   - Day 0: Deliver the free report
   - Day 1: "Una cosa que no te contamos en el informe..."
   - Day 3: Case study of a similar business that improved their score
   - Day 5: Workshop invitation or Pro report offer with countdown
   - Day 7: Last chance / scarcity ("Solo 15 plazas en el taller")
3. **Retargeting ads** to people who completed the free tool but didn't buy

### Psychological Triggers
- **Reciprocity** — "We already gave you a free report. This is the complete version."
- **Commitment & consistency** — They already invested time in the free tool. Small payment is the next logical step.
- **Scarcity** — Workshop limited to 15 people. Pro report "personalized" (implies limited availability).
- **Specificity** — "15 paginas" / "30-day calendar" / "15 minutes of video" — concrete, tangible deliverables.

- **Standard path**: Free tool → Tripwire → Starter → Core in 4-8 months

## 4. SALES PROCESS & CONVERSION PLAYBOOK

### For Rungs 0-2 (No-Touch / Low-Touch Sales)

**Entirely online, self-service, automated:**

1. **Traffic** → brutalmk.com (Meta Ads, Google Ads, organic content, referrals)
2. **Free tool completion** → Immediate email with results
3. **Email nurture sequence** (7 days, 5 emails) → Tripwire offer
4. **Post-purchase sequence** (after Tripwire) → Starter plan CTA
5. **Payment** via Stripe or similar → Onboarding email with Notion board access

**Tools needed:** Email automation (ConvertKit, Mailchimp, or Brevo), Stripe, Notion/ClickUp, Meta Ads, Google Ads

**Who manages:** Antonio (automation + tool), Jose (email copy), Ana (ad creatives)

### For Rungs 3-4 (Consultative Sales)

**The Discovery Call Framework (30 min):**

1. **Context** (5 min): "Tell me about your business. What are you working toward this year?"
2. **Pain** (5 min): "What's your biggest frustration with your current marketing?"
3. **Impact** (5 min): "How much revenue are you leaving on the table because of this?"
4. **Vision** (5 min): "If we could solve this in 6 months, what would that look like for your business?"
5. **Fit** (5 min): Present the relevant package. "Based on what you've told me, Estrategia SEO & AEO at €1,200/mes would address exactly this."
6. **Next step** (5 min): "I'll send you a custom proposal by Friday. If it makes sense, we start next Monday."

**Rules:**
- Never give a price on the first call if they skipped Rungs 0-2. Say: "Let me put together something specific for you."
- Always follow up within 24 hours with a proposal
- Proposal = 3-5 page custom document, not a generic PDF
- Include a "do nothing" scenario: "If you continue as-is, here's what the next 12 months look like..."

### For Rung 5 (Relationship Sales)

- No formal sales process. This emerges organically from 6-12 months of working together.
- Jose or Xino recognizes the opportunity and creates it.
- In-person meeting (lunch, dinner, or office visit).
- Custom partnership agreement drafted after verbal commitment.

## 5. RETENTION & ANTI-CHURN SYSTEM

### Why Clients Leave Agencies (And How BRUTAL. Prevents It)

| Reason | Industry % | BRUTAL.'s Prevention |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|
| Perceived indifference | 40% | Biweekly calls, Slack access, proactive "we noticed X" messages |
| No visible results | 25% | Monthly reports with clear KPIs; celebrate wins publicly |
| Communication breakdown | 20% | Async Notion board + defined response times per rung |
| Price vs. value mismatch | 10% | Quarterly ROI reviews showing €-for-€ return |
| Better offer elsewhere | 5% | AEO niche = hard to replace; deep business knowledge = switching cost |

### The BRUTAL. Retention Playbook

#### 1. The First 45 Days (Make or Break)
- **Day 1:** Welcome Loom video from their strategist + onboarding questionnaire
- **Day 3:** Kickoff call (30 min) — set expectations, establish KPIs, define communication cadence
- **Day 7:** First deliverable (even if small) — early win builds confidence
- **Day 14:** Check-in call — "How are you feeling? Any questions?"
- **Day 30:** First monthly report — show activity AND early results
- **Day 45:** Strategy adjustment call — "Here's what we've learned in 45 days and what we're changing"

#### 2. Ongoing Retention Rituals
- **Monthly report** sent 24 hours before the monthly call (so the client reads it first)
- **Quarterly business review (QBR):** Deep-dive into ROI, strategy for next quarter, and competitive landscape update. This is where you resell the value.
- **Annual strategy day:** For Rung 4-5 clients, a half-day workshop to plan the coming year
- **Birthday/anniversary touchpoints:** Remember client's business anniversary, personal milestones

#### 3. The "Surprise & Delight" System
Once per quarter, deliver something unexpected and unasked for:
- A competitive analysis they didn't request
- A new landing page design concept
- An industry trend report relevant to their sector
- A referral to a potential client for THEIR business

#### 4. Contract Structure That Reduces Churn
- **Rung 2:** 3-month minimum, then month-to-month
- **Rung 3:** 6-month minimum, then month-to-month. Offer 10% discount for annual commitment.
- **Rung 4:** 6-month minimum, then month-to-month. Offer 15% discount for annual commitment.
- **Rung 5:** 12-month contract with quarterly review checkpoints

#### 5. Exit Prevention Protocol
When a client signals potential churn (missed calls, reduced communication, complaints):
1. **Jose or Xino calls personally** (not the regular point of contact) within 24 hours
2. **Listen first.** Don't defend. Don't offer discounts immediately.
3. **Identify the real issue.** Is it results? Communication? Budget? Changed priorities?
4. **Propose a fix** — not a discount. Adjust strategy, increase touchpoints, shift focus.
5. **If they must leave:** Make it graceful. Offer a transition plan. Leave the door open. 60% of lost clients return within 12 months if the exit was handled well.

### Target Retention Metrics
- Monthly churn rate: <3% (industry average: 5-10%)
- Average client lifetime: 14+ months (industry average: 8-12 months)
- Client NPS: 60+ (industry average: 30-40)
